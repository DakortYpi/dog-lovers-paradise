Dog Care Tips

• Nap time is serious business: If your dog is snoring, let them be. Disturbing their nap is like waking a bear from hibernation.

• Barking is a form of communication: If your dog is barking excessively, try asking them what they want. You might be surprised at how articulate they can be.

• Treats are a form of currency: Use them wisely to bribe your dog into doing anything, from going for a walk to sitting still for a bath.

• Dogs are masters of disguise: They can turn a puddle into a mud bath in seconds. Be prepared.

• Never underestimate the power of a belly rub: It's the ultimate form of canine therapy.

• If you're ever lost in the woods, your dog will find you: They have an uncanny ability to sense your location, even if you're hiding under a bush.

• Dogs are the ultimate judges of character: If your dog doesn't like someone, there's probably a good reason.

• Never dress your dog in clothes: Unless it's a sweater on a cold day. Dogs are not fashion accessories.

• Always carry a spare leash: You never know when your dog might decide to take a detour.

Remember, dogs are family: Treat them with love, respect, and a never-ending supply of treats.
